Friendly Twitterbot

Twitter can be depressing sometimes--that's why we created a bot that keeps an eye on what you're viewing and tweeting, and checks in with you if it thinks your feed may be putting you in a bad mood.

What does this bot do?

This bot keeps an eye on what you're viewing on Twitter (as well as the tweets you're writing) and uses sentiment analysis to try and figure out if your feed is overly negative. If it thinks your Twitter feed is too depressing, it'll send you a check-in email or text with some recommended resources to help you deal with whatever you're feeling (eg. positive news, meditation/mindfulness tips, etc).

Get started!

Enter your twitter handle here (needs to be a public account, or the bot won't be able to view/monitor your tweets) and our bot will start monitoring your account and keeping tabs on what you post!

We'll never share your email with anyone else.

How does it work?

The bot keeps an eye on two things: what you view and what you post. It keeps track of what you view by going through your following list and running sentiment analysis on these users' Twitter feeds (since their tweets are a large portion of what occupies your feed).

It keeps track of what you post by scraping your recent tweets and running sentiment analysis on them.

Greater emphasis is placed on what you tweet, but since most people tend to view more than they tweet, we figure it's still very important to consider what's being tweeted by the people you follow.
